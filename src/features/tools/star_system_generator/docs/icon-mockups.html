Rogue / Captured — alternatives

Three variants, all built around a proper planet-sized disc (~r=7) so it reads as a planet first.

Variant 24 px 16 px Concept
A — trajectory Full cratered planet on a dashed trajectory line that resumes on the other side. "Captured into a passing path."
B — motion trail Planet shifted right with two short motion strokes trailing behind. Suggests an interstellar interloper still in transit.
C — eccentric orbit Planet wrapped by a dashed, tilted elliptical orbit. "Captured into a non-standard orbit." Slight risk of confusion with ice-giant — different tilt direction (35° vs -72°) and dashed vs solid help distinguish.

Available Lucide planet/globe-adjacent icons

Every relevant icon shipped in lucide-react@^1.7.0. Note: Globe2 is a re-export of Earth (same icon). Stars is a re-export of Sparkles.

Name 24 px 16 px What it is / fit for terrestrial worlds
Globe Circle + meridian curve + equator line. Reads as an abstract globe — no continents. Could fit super-earth as a more "schematic" planet.
Globe2 (= Earth) Currently used. Disc with continent contours. Strong "Earth-like" read.
EarthLock Earth + a "+" symbol. Not a great fit — semantic clash with "lock/secured".
Atom Center dot + two crossed elliptical orbits. Reads as "atom," not planet. Skip.
Orbit Already used in OrbitalTable. Central body + two orbiting smaller bodies + arcs. Reads as "system," not "planet."
Moon Crescent moon. Already used in BodyDetailPanel for moons specifically.
Satellite Spacecraft satellite. Reads as artificial, not a body. Skip.
Star 5-pointed star. Reads as "star/favorite," not planet. Skip.

Conclusion: Globe2 (Earth) and the original Globe are the only true planet-readable Lucide icons. Globe2 stays for super-earth as you preferred. The remaining custom-SVG candidates are still rocky-planet, sub-neptune, gas-giant, ice-giant, and rogue-captured (variant A).

Summary

  • new 5 custom SVGs: rocky-planet, sub-neptune, gas-giant, ice-giant, rogue-captured (variant A)
  • keep 4 Lucide icons: Globe2 (super-earth), CircleDashed (belt), CircleSmall (dwarf-body), Sparkles (anomaly)
  • All collisions resolved. Each category has a unique silhouette readable at 16px.
